Household

        
Marital status, filing postures, and the names that appear on each return.

        
Names and marital status drive filing-status logic on both sides. The Finanzamt and the IRS each compare names to your tax IDs, so the values here should match your government IDs exactly.

Non-U.S. accounts for Schedule B Part III and FBAR thresholds.

Vorabpauschale Idle

        
Per-fund inputs for the InvStG § 18 advance lump-sum calculation.

        

          Per-fund inputs feeding InvStG § 18 Vorabpauschale. NAV at first

Prior-Year Carryovers Idle

        
Foreign tax credit, capital loss, and § 20 Abs. 6 carryforwards.

        

          U.S. FTC carryovers per 26 U.S.C. § 904(c), capital-loss
          carryovers per 26 U.S.C. § 1212(b), and German § 20 Abs. 6

Children & Dependents Idle

        
One row per child or dependent, used by both U.S. and German credits.

        

          One row per child or dependent. The IRS uses these rows for the
          Child Tax Credit and the Credit for Other Dependents
          (26 U.S.C. § 24, § 152). Germany uses them for the
          Kinderfreibetrag / Kindergeld Günstigerprüfung
          (§ 31, § 32 EStG; BKGG § 6).
